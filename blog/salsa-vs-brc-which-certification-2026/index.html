SALSA vs BRC: Which Food Safety Certification Does Your Startup Need?

Food startups get confused between SALSA and BRC. Both are food safety certifications. Both cost money. Both take time. But they're not the same. Here's which one you actually need.

What's SALSA?

SALSA (Safe & Local Supplier Approval) is managed by the SALSA scheme. It's specifically for food manufacturers wanting to sell to **major UK retailers** (Tesco, Waitrose, Ocado, etc.).

SALSA checks:

  • Food safety controls are in place
  • Your facility is clean and documented
  • You have proper traceability systems
  • You understand allergen management

What's BRC?

BRC (British Retail Consortium) is more rigorous. It's a food safety standard that's globally recognized and required by many large retailers and food manufacturers who supply them.

BRC checks:

  • Everything SALSA does, plus:
  • Supplier verification (you audit YOUR suppliers)
  • Preventive controls for allergens, foreign objects, chemical contamination
  • Documentation & audit trails (much stricter)
  • Management system depth (more paperwork)

Which One Do YOU Need?

Choose SALSA if:
• You want to sell to Tesco, Sainsbury's, Waitrose, Ocado
• You're starting out (cheaper, faster)
• You make pickles, jams, sauces in small batches
• You're UK-based with proper facilities
Choose BRC if:
• You're manufacturing at scale (5,000+ units/month)
• You supply other manufacturers or big distributors
• You want global credibility (BRC is worldwide recognized)
• You plan to scale beyond UK retailers

The Real Talk

Most food startups should start with SALSA. It's cheaper, faster, and gets you into the major UK retailers. Once you're hitting serious volume and scaling beyond just selling pickles at farmers markets, THEN you move to BRC.

Doing both is expensive and pointless unless you're already manufacturing at scale.

What About Organic or Specialty Certifications?

Organic: Separate from SALSA/BRC. Required only if you claim "organic" on packaging. Adds 3-6 months and £1,200+. Worth it if your brand story depends on it.

Vegan/Kosher/Halal: Not food safety certifications—they're marketing claims. Don't need formal certification unless you're selling to specific retailers who demand it.

Cost & Timeline for SALSA

  • Assessment cost: £800-£1,200
  • Preparation: 4-6 weeks (get your facility audit-ready)
  • Audit day: 1 day on site
  • Results: 2-3 weeks after audit
  • Annual renewal: £400-£600 per year
